<h2>Speech &amp; Prize-Giving Day</h2>
<p>On 11 April 2026, Changkat Changi Secondary School marked a significant
milestone with our 60<sup>th</sup> Annual Speech and Prize-Giving Day. Centred
on the theme ‘Thrive Together, Inspire Excellence’, the ceremony brought
together students, staff, parents, alumni and community partners to celebrate
the achievements of our students and reflect on six decades of growth.</p>

<p>The school was honoured to welcome our Guest of Honour, Mr Lim Guan Yu,
A.P.A.C. Chief Engineer at KX Services, First Derivative/E.P.A.M., and
President of the Changkat Changi Secondary School Alumni Association, to
grace the occasion. A proud alumnus, his journey from Changkat to where
he stands today embodies what the school stands for. &nbsp;Our Principal,
Mr Kwok Ying Liang, shared stories of students who have gone above and
beyond in service, in sport, and in their pursuit of excellence. Mr Lim's
address that followed was equally inspiring, leaving students and guests
with much to reflect on.</p>

<p>The Concert Band then took to the stage, performing 'Choose Joy' by Randall
Standridge, a piece inspired by a young student who chose to find joy in
the face of adversity. Their performance was warmly received, and carried
added significance as the band prepares to make their debut at the Asia
Pacific Band Festival.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Changkat 60/spgd5.png">
</div>
<p>The ceremony honoured students who excelled at the 2025 GCE O-Level, N(A)-Level
and N(T)-Level examinations, with prizes presented across individual subjects
and special scholarships. The LCS Ji Yang Education Award, Neo Choon Tiong
Scholarship, Qaisara Scholarship for Student Leadership and Ivy Ho Arts
Bursary Award were among the accolades presented to deserving recipients.</p>
<p>A highlight of the afternoon was the valedictory address by Rico Tan Jun
Hong of 4B (2025), the Overall Best Student of the 2025 cohort. As Student
Council President, national-level Track and Field athlete, and recipient
of multiple academic prizes, Rico exemplified the Changkat spirit of being
resilient, service-oriented and purpose-driven. His words resonated deeply
with the audience, offering an inspiring close to the prize presentations.</p>

<p>The 60<sup>th</sup> Annual Speech and Prize-Giving Day was a proud celebration
of our school community of students who strived, teachers who guided, and
families who supported them. As we continue our anniversary year, the ceremony
reminded us of what has always driven Changkat: the belief that excellence
is nurtured over time, together.</p>
